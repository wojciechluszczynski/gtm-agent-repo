# Skill 04 — Executive Dossier

Use when: high-value account, you want deep research on one specific person
before a call, a demo, or a strategic outreach.
Input: name + company + role
Output: structured one-page dossier in markdown
Runtime: 10–15 minutes per person

---

## What a dossier contains

1. Professional background (current role, tenure, previous companies)
2. Public statements (LinkedIn posts, interviews, podcast appearances, quotes)
3. Priorities and pressures (what they are publicly focused on)
4. Likely pain points based on role + company stage + ICP signals
5. Conversation openers (3 specific, researched angles)
6. What NOT to say (based on their stated preferences or public positions)

---

## Prompt

Task:
Build an executive dossier for:
- Name: [Full name]
- Title: [Job title]
- Company: [Company name]
- Context: [1 sentence — why we are researching this person]

Research sources to check:
1. LinkedIn profile (posts, activity, career history)
2. Company website (leadership page, press quotes)
3. Podcast appearances or interviews (search "[name] podcast" or "[name] interview")
4. Published articles or guest posts
5. Conference speaker appearances
6. Recent company news that affects their role

Produce a dossier with these sections:

## Background
[2–3 sentences: current role, how long, where before]

## What they talk about publicly
[3 bullet points with source URLs]

## Inferred priorities for this quarter
[Based on company stage, recent hires, product launches, market signals]

## Likely pain points
[Mapped to our BATTLECARD.md — what problems do we solve that are relevant to their role]

## 3 conversation openers
[Specific, researched, non-generic. Each with the source that inspired it.]

## What to avoid
[Topics, framings, or approaches likely to create friction based on their public positions]

Export: save to clients/<client>/dossiers/<name>-<date>.md
